An end-to-end **Business Intelligence (BI)** project that analyzes the economic performance of the **BRICS nations (Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa)** using **World Bank Open Data (2000–2024)**.

This project demonstrates the complete BI lifecycle—from **data extraction and ETL automation** to **SQL data modeling**, **Power BI dashboard development**, and **executive-level business analysis**.

---

# 📌 Project Overview

The objective of this project is to transform raw macroeconomic data into actionable business intelligence that helps governments, policymakers, investors, researchers, and business leaders understand the long-term economic performance of BRICS countries.

The dashboard analyzes **15 macroeconomic indicators** over **25 years (2000–2024)** and provides executive-level insights into growth, investment, trade, inflation, employment, fiscal health, and economic resilience.

---

# 🎯 Project Objectives

- Analyze long-term economic trends across BRICS nations.
- Compare country-level economic performance.
- Identify investment opportunities and economic risks.
- Evaluate macroeconomic stability.
- Support data-driven decision making through interactive dashboards.
- Deliver executive-level business insights backed by real data.

# 📌 Major Business Insights

### 📈 BRICS Economic Growth

- Combined BRICS GDP increased from **USD 2.76 Trillion** in 2000 to **USD 27.41 Trillion** in 2024.
- Overall economic expansion reached **893%** over 25 years.

---

### 🇨🇳 China

- Largest contributor to total BRICS GDP.
- Generated a **USD 534 Billion Trade Surplus**.
- Experienced a significant decline in FDI after 2021.
- Remains the manufacturing hub of BRICS.

---

### 🇮🇳 India

- Fastest-growing major economy.
- GDP increased by approximately **735%**.
- Largest improvement in Life Expectancy.
- Strong digital transformation and manufacturing growth.

---

### 🇷🇺 Russia

- Lowest Government Debt among BRICS.
- Lowest unemployment rate.
- Declining FDI due to geopolitical challenges.
- Strong fiscal discipline despite external risks.

---

### 🇧🇷 Brazil

- Second-highest FDI inflows.
- Rising Government Debt remains a long-term challenge.
- Strong agricultural and commodity exports.

---

### 🇿🇦 South Africa

- Highest unemployment rate within BRICS.
- Faces persistent structural labour market challenges.
- Slowest long-term economic growth.

---

# ⚠ Business Problems Identified

The analysis identified six major macroeconomic challenges:

- High unemployment in South Africa
- China's sharp decline in FDI inflows
- Russia's dependence on oil exports
- India's persistent trade deficit
- Brazil's increasing government debt
- COVID-19 economic disruption across BRICS
